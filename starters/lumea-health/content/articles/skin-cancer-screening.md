Skin cancer rates in Poland have been rising steadily, with melanoma incidence doubling over the past two decades. Early detection through regular skin screenings is the single most effective way to improve outcomes.

## The ABCDE Rule

When examining your moles, watch for these warning signs:

- **A — Asymmetry**: One half doesn't match the other
- **B — Border**: Irregular, ragged, or blurred edges
- **C — Color**: Multiple colors or uneven distribution
- **D — Diameter**: Larger than 6mm (pencil eraser size)
- **E — Evolution**: Any change in size, shape, or color

## Who Should Get Screened

Annual skin checks are recommended for people who:
- Have more than 50 moles
- Have a family history of melanoma
- Have had severe sunburns, especially in childhood
- Have fair skin and light eyes
- Use or have used tanning beds
