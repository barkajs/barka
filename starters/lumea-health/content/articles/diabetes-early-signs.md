Diabetes affects over 3 million people in Poland, and it's estimated that around 1 million of them are undiagnosed. Type 2 diabetes develops gradually, and early symptoms are often subtle and easy to dismiss.

## Warning Signs

- **Increased thirst and frequent urination** — Your kidneys work overtime to filter excess glucose
- **Unexplained fatigue** — Your cells aren't getting the energy they need
- **Blurred vision** — High blood sugar affects the lens of your eye
- **Slow wound healing** — Elevated glucose impairs circulation and immune function
- **Numbness or tingling in hands/feet** — Early nerve damage from chronic high blood sugar
- **Darkened skin patches** — Acanthosis nigricans, especially in skin folds

## Who Is at Risk

- Family history of diabetes
- Overweight or obese (BMI > 25)
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Age over 45
- History of gestational diabetes
- Polycystic ovary syndrome

## The Importance of Early Detection

A simple fasting blood glucose test can detect diabetes or pre-diabetes. When caught early, Type 2 diabetes can often be managed — and sometimes reversed — through lifestyle changes alone.
