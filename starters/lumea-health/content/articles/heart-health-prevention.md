Cardiovascular disease is responsible for approximately 45% of all deaths in Poland, making it the nation's leading killer. The good news? Most heart disease is preventable through lifestyle modifications and regular health monitoring.

## 1. Move for 30 Minutes Daily

You don't need to run marathons. A brisk 30-minute walk, five days a week, reduces your heart disease risk by up to 35%. Swimming, cycling, or even dancing counts. The key is consistency, not intensity.

## 2. Follow a Mediterranean-Style Diet

Research consistently shows that a diet rich in vegetables, fruits, whole grains, olive oil, and fish provides significant cardiovascular protection. Limit processed foods, excessive salt, and added sugars.

## 3. Know Your Numbers

Regular health check-ups should include blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood glucose measurements. Many people with hypertension or high cholesterol have no symptoms — that's why screening is crucial.

## 4. Manage Stress Actively

Chronic stress contributes to inflammation and unhealthy coping behaviors. Find what works for you: meditation, nature walks, yoga, or simply setting boundaries at work. Your heart will thank you.

## 5. Don't Skip Sleep

Adults who consistently sleep fewer than 6 hours per night have a significantly higher risk of heart disease. Aim for 7-8 hours of quality sleep and address issues like sleep apnea, which is an independent cardiovascular risk factor.
