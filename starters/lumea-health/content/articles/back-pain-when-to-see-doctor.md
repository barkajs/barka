Back pain affects 80% of adults at some point in their lives. While most episodes resolve within a few weeks with basic self-care, some symptoms indicate a more serious condition that requires professional evaluation.

## Red Flags — See a Doctor Immediately

- Pain following a fall or injury
- Numbness or tingling in your legs
- Loss of bladder or bowel control
- Pain that worsens at night or doesn't improve with rest
- Unexplained weight loss alongside back pain
- Fever accompanying back pain

## Self-Care for Mild Back Pain

- Stay active — bed rest is not recommended
- Apply heat or cold packs
- Over-the-counter pain relief (ibuprofen, paracetamol)
- Gentle stretching and movement
- Maintain good posture, especially when sitting
