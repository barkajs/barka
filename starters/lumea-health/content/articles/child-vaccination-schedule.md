Vaccinations are one of the most effective tools we have to protect children from serious infectious diseases. Poland has a well-established National Immunization Programme that covers essential vaccines at no cost.

## Mandatory Vaccines in Poland

The following vaccinations are mandatory (free of charge):
- **Newborn**: Hepatitis B, BCG (tuberculosis)
- **2-6 months**: DTaP (diphtheria, tetanus, pertussis), IPV (polio), Hib, Hepatitis B
- **13-15 months**: MMR (measles, mumps, rubella)
- **6 years**: DTaP booster, MMR booster
- **14 years**: Td booster

## Recommended (Optional) Vaccines

These are available at an additional cost and strongly recommended:
- Rotavirus (from 6 weeks)
- Pneumococcal (from 6 weeks)
- Meningococcal B and C
- Varicella (chickenpox)
- HPV (from age 9)

## Tips for Parents

- Keep a vaccination record book and bring it to every appointment
- Mild fever and irritability after vaccination are normal
- Contact your pediatrician if your child has a high fever (>39°C) lasting more than 48 hours
